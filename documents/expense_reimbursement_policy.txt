EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Last Updated: April 10, 2025

1. GENERAL GUIDELINES
All business-related expenses must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager before being incurred. Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred. Late submissions (beyond 30 days) require director-level approval and may be subject to denial. All expense claims must be accompanied by original receipts or digital copies.

2. TRAVEL EXPENSES
Domestic flights must be booked in economy class. Business class is permitted for international flights exceeding 6 hours. Hotel accommodations are reimbursed up to $200 per night for domestic travel and $300 per night for international travel. Rental cars are reimbursed for standard or intermediate class only. Employees using personal vehicles for business travel will be reimbursed at $0.67 per mile.

3. MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT
Meal expenses during business travel are reimbursed up to the following daily limits: breakfast $20, lunch $30, dinner $50. Client entertainment expenses must be pre-approved and are limited to $150 per person per event. Alcohol is reimbursable only during client-facing meals and is limited to $50 per person. Tips are reimbursable up to 20% of the pre-tax bill amount.

4. TECHNOLOGY AND SOFTWARE
Software subscriptions required for work must be approved by IT and the employee's manager. Hardware purchases (monitors, keyboards, headsets) up to $200 do not require pre-approval. Hardware purchases exceeding $200 require IT and manager approval. The company provides a $100 monthly stipend for mobile phone expenses for employees who use personal devices for work.

5. PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
The company provides an annual professional development budget of $2,000 per employee. This covers conference attendance, online courses, certifications, and books. Conference travel expenses are covered separately under the travel policy. Unused professional development funds do not carry over to the next year.

6. SUBMISSION PROCESS
All expense reports must be submitted through the Expensify platform. Required information includes: date of expense, vendor name, amount, business purpose, and project code. Receipts must be attached as photos or scanned copies. Manager approval is required within 5 business days of submission. Approved expenses are reimbursed in the next payroll cycle.

7. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
The following expenses are not eligible for reimbursement: personal travel or entertainment, gym memberships, clothing (unless safety equipment), fines or penalties (e.g., parking tickets, traffic violations), first-class air travel (unless medically required with documentation), expenses without valid receipts, and commuting costs between home and regular office.

8. CORPORATE CREDIT CARDS
Corporate credit cards are available for employees at the manager level and above. Monthly statements must be reconciled within 10 business days of the statement date. Personal charges on corporate cards must be repaid within 5 business days. Failure to reconcile statements may result in card suspension.
