CODE OF CONDUCT
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Last Updated: March 20, 2025

1. PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
All employees are expected to conduct themselves with integrity, respect, and professionalism at all times. This applies to interactions with colleagues, clients, vendors, and the public. Employees represent the company both during and outside of working hours when engaging in company-related activities.

2. ANTI-DISCRIMINATION AND HARASSMENT
The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for discrimination and harassment based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic. Harassment includes but is not limited to: unwelcome verbal or physical conduct, offensive jokes or comments, intimidation, and bullying. All complaints will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

3. REPORTING VIOLATIONS
Employees who witness or experience violations of this code should report them through any of the following channels: direct manager, HR department, anonymous ethics hotline (1-800-555-ETHICS), or online reporting portal at ethics.company.com. Retaliation against employees who report violations in good faith is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their manager and the ethics committee. This includes outside employment, financial interests in competitors or vendors, and personal relationships with colleagues that may affect business decisions. Employees may not accept gifts valued at more than $50 from vendors or clients without prior approval.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY
Employees must protect company confidential information including trade secrets, financial data, customer information, and strategic plans. Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends. Sharing confidential information with unauthorized parties may result in termination and legal action. All employees must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon joining the company.

6. USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES
Company resources including equipment, software, and facilities are provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted as long as it does not interfere with work duties or violate any company policies. Employees must not use company resources for illegal activities, personal business ventures, or political campaigning.

7. SOCIAL MEDIA
Employees must not share confidential company information on social media platforms. When posting about the company, employees must clearly state that their views are personal and do not represent the company. Employees should not engage in online behavior that could damage the company's reputation. The marketing department must approve any official company social media communications.

8. DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
Violations of this code of conduct may result in disciplinary action ranging from verbal warning to termination depending on the severity. The disciplinary process includes: verbal warning, written warning, suspension without pay, and termination. Serious violations such as fraud, theft, or violence may result in immediate termination without prior warnings. All disciplinary actions will be documented in the employee's personnel file.

9. SUBSTANCE ABUSE
The company maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, or distribution of illegal substances on company premises or during work hours is prohibited. Employees must not report to work under the influence of alcohol or drugs. The company offers an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for employees seeking help with substance abuse issues.

10. ACKNOWLEDGMENT
All employees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge this Code of Conduct annually. Acknowledgment is completed through the HR portal during the annual compliance training cycle. New employees must acknowledge the code during their onboarding process within the first week of employment.
